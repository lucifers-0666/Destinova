Definitions Glossary

Understanding Key Terms

Booking Reference

A unique alphanumeric code (6 characters) assigned to your reservation. Also known as PNR (Passenger Name Record). Use this to manage your booking online or at the airport.

Example: ABC123

Fare Class

A letter code that determines ticket rules, restrictions, and benefits. Economy classes typically use letters like Y, M, B, H. Higher letters (Y) usually offer more flexibility.

Example: Economy Y (flexible), Economy B (restricted)

PNR (Passenger Name Record)

A record stored in the airline's reservation system containing all details of your journey: passenger names, flight segments, contact information, special requests, and payment status.

GDS (Global Distribution System)

A computerized network system that enables real-time booking of airline seats, hotel rooms, and car rentals. Major GDS providers include Amadeus, Sabre, and Travelport.

Layover vs Stopover

Layover: Short connection between flights (typically under 24 hours) without leaving the airport.

Stopover: Extended stay (over 24 hours) at a connecting city, often requiring separate accommodation.

Codeshare Flight

A flight operated by one airline but marketed and sold by another airline under its own flight code. You book with Airline A, but Airline B operates the actual flight.

Example: Book AI 123 (Air India), fly on LH 456 (Lufthansa)

Scheduled Departure Time

The official time when the aircraft is scheduled to leave the gate (not when it takes off). This is the time printed on your ticket and used for check-in deadlines.

Check-in Deadline

The latest time by which you must complete check-in procedures. Typically 2 hours before domestic flights and 3 hours before international flights. Late arrivals forfeit boarding rights.
